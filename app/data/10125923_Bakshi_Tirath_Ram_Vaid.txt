Sardar Bahadur Risaldar Major and Honorary Captain Bakshi Tirath Ram Vaid, (1857–1924), also known as Tirath Ram, was a decorated soldier of the British Indian Army. Vaid enlisted in the ranks of the Queen's Own Corps of Guides on 1 May 1876.January 1908 Indian Army List As a ressaidar with the Queen's Own Corps of Guides cavalry, Vaid was awarded the Indian Order of Merit, Third Class for gallantry displayed at the Siege of Malakand in 1897, where he fought against a much larger force of Afghan tribesmen.January 1908 Indian Army List He was also rewarded with large tracts of agricultural land in District Lyallpur for his contributions. Vaid was decorated with the Order of British India, Second Class, with the title of Bahadur on 24 September 1904.January 1908 Indian Army List He retired from the Indian Army and was appointed honorary captain on 1 February 1909.THE LONDON GAZETTE, 15 JUNE, 1909, page 4578 ==References== Category:1857 births Category:1924 deaths Category:British Indian Army officers Category:Punjabi people Category:Punjabi Hindus Category:Recipients of the Indian Order of Merit
